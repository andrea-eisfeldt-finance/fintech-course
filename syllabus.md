# Syllabus

## Course Format

Class consists of lectures, cases, group presentations, and guest speakers. Readings marked with an asterisk (*) are required prior to the date of class discussion.

## Course Materials

**Required:** [FinTech Case Pack](https://hbsp.harvard.edu/import/1387361)

Required and optional readings are linked on each weekly topic page.

## Evaluation and Grading

| Assignment | Weight |
|-----------|--------|
| Group Business Proposal (Part I: 10%, Part II: 10%, Part III: 15%) | 30% |
| 2 Group Cases (includes 7% peer review) | 30% |
| 1 Individual Case | 15% |
| Class Participation | 25% |
| **Total** | **100%** |

## Assignments

### Case Assignments

See the [Case Assignment Guidelines]({% link case-guidelines.md %}) for complete format and grading details.

Cases marked "A" or "B" are due on the date of the class discussion for groups in the A or B groups, respectively. Case questions are posted on the course site the week prior to the due date. Groups are assigned by program (MBA, FEMBA, EMBA).

- [Purchase case pack here](https://hbsp.harvard.edu/import/1387361)

### Individual Case: Case of Unidentified Financial Firms

Due Week 5. This individual assignment asks you to identify financial institutions from disguised balance sheet and income statement data, applying concepts from Weeks 1–3.

### Business Proposal Project

The capstone project, worth 30% of your grade. See the full [Business Proposal Assignment]({% link business-proposal.md %}) for detailed descriptions, examples, grading criteria, resources, and guidance on using AI tools.

**Summary of three parts:**
- **Part I — Industry Analysis (10%):** Five-slide summary of your chosen fintech sub-industry (Due: Week 3 for Group A, Week 4 for Group B)
- **Part II — Incumbent Case Study (10%):** Five-slide incumbent firm analysis (Due: Week 6 for Group A, Week 7 for Group B)
- **Part III — Business Proposal (15%):** 10 slides maximum with financial statements (Presentations: Week 9 for Group A, Week 10 for Group B; Final written proposal due June 16)

### Class Participation

A substantial part of your participation grade is based on in-class discussions.
